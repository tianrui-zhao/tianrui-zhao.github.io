title: "The “contractor + indentured worker” system from a principal-agent perspective: evidence from the labor market in modern Shanghai"
collection: publications
category: manuscripts
excerpt: 'During the industrial transformation of modern Shanghai, the “contractor + indentured worker” system emerged as a localized and pre-modern governance mechanism aimed at reducing transaction costs. Embedded in networks of kinship and native-place ties, this system played a crucial role in stabilizing labor relations and mitigating disputes between employers and workers. This paper constructs a dual principal-agent model involving entrepreneurs, labor contractors, and workers. The results show that informal institutions and incomplete contracts can serve as mechanisms for information screening, enabling a separating equilibrium that matches workers to tasks according to their abilities, thus maximizing production efficiency. The theoretical findings are further supported by empirical analysis using historical data from the labor market in modern Shanghai. This study also offers valuable insights for understanding the evolution of labor relations in China since the reform and opening-up period.'
